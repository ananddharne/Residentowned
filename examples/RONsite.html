Introduction

The concept of Resident Owned Neighbourhoods builds on the work of Resident Owned Communities USA (ROC USA), which pioneerered a shift in the ownership of mobile home parks from corporations into resident owned communities. ROC USA has transformed almost 200 mobile home neighbourhoods, demonstrating that, with the support system provided by their organization, residents are reliable in paying their mortgages and successfully managing the ownership of the property. ROC USA has mastered the financial efforts necessary to create resident ownership, but they haven’t infused these efforts with the social capital building and the health improvement strategies that Insightformation is facilitating with places like Communities of Hope. In addition, Insightformation is an innovator in areas of community collaboration and teamwork, which are necessary to create a vibrant and connected community.

Current Practices

Current efforts to try to stabilize neighbourhoods do not tackle the root of the problem: shifting as many low-income people as possible into home ownership. 35 percent of African American households spend fifty percent of their income on housing. But, just like a number of the people in the mobile home parks probably would not be successful if they just bought out the property, they wouldn’t be able to buy out the property by themselves and they probably wouldn’t be able to manage it without help. It’s the same, we need to bring the appropriate assists to create RON. But in bringing that we think there’s a huge number of benefits over a lot of what’s currently being done.

Being homeless is obviously something they want to avoid, so paying for housing is going to be their first investment and if they get squeezed to spend more and more, other things just get squeezed out, not to mention quality of life and everything else. But, if you could switch that over to ownership where the payment, some of it is taxes, but some of it is interest to a friendly local nonprofit foundation type lender and a big chunk of it is principle, no they’re building assets even if they’re not great financial managers because they’re building the assets in the property. And that is life changing. We want to multiply that by taking a very different approach.

The land bank, the community land thrust, those are steps in the right direction and we can certainly learn from them. Habitat for humanity is starting to work toward broader neighbourhood revitalising, focusing on entire neighbourhoods.
